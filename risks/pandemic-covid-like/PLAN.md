## Mitigation approaches

### Fast Grants

The Collison/Cowen project was the most effective fast civilian intervention early in the COVID pandemic (mid-April 2020). Fast Grants funded a few dozen researchers on extremely short notice, including notably the ability to run trial tests in Brazil to circumvent American legalese.

It did *not* accelerate vaccine manufacture and distribution timelines, and vaccine timelines were far more pivotal than anything else. Fast Grants' most successful piece of funded research was probably the Yale team that enabled cheaper-than-swab saliva-based [tests](https://future.com/what-we-learned-doing-fast-grants/#:~:text=The%20SalivaDirect%20team%20at%20Yale%20demonstrated%20that%20saliva%2Dbased%20COVID%2D19%20tests%20can%20work%20just%20as%20well%20as%20those%20using%20nasopharyngeal%20swabs.%20This%20work%20was%20critical%20to%20ameliorate%20the%20swab%20and%20trained%20clinician%20\(for%20swab%2Dbased%20test%20administration\)%20shortages%20at%20the%20outset%20of%20the%20pandemic.).

More than half the researchers Fast Grants funded claimed their research wouldn't have happened without it, and the rest say it would've taken another few months to get the same results via traditional funding routes. (See writeup [here](https://future.com/what-we-learned-doing-fast-grants/).)

### Fast Grants could have happened earlier

There are [multiple](https://www.science.org/content/article/nih-grapples-researchers-rush-claim-billions-pandemic-research-funds#:~:text=In%20March%2C%20she%20penned%20a%20commentary%20pleading%20for%20the%20government%20to%20give%20funding%20to%20universities%20that%20they%20could%20award%20as%20seed%20grants.%20Absent%20a%20response%2C%20she%20has%20helped%20her%20university%20raise%20money%20through%20philanthropy%20to%20provide%20seed%20funding%20for%2019%20projects%20so%20far.) cases in early March 2020 of researchers loudly complaining about the lack of funding around COVID despite obvious urgencyâ€”COVID was already no longer [low-status](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/5okDRahtDewnWfFmz/seeing-the-smoke) and researchers were scrambling to respond to it. This means that **Fast Grants could plausibly have happened 4-6 weeks earlier than it did.**

There was researcher demand; the WHO declared COVID a pandemic 27 days before Fast Grants started handing out money; the Italian outbreak that spread panic in the world happened in *February* 2020. Fast Grants was, if anything, slow on the uptake.
